How to avoid hotel booking fees in Australia

Surprise fees and inflated “from” prices are the worst part of booking a hotel. Here’s how they work - and how to pay the real price.

What “booking fees” actually are

When a hotel room costs more than it should, the extra usually comes from one of a few places: a booking site’s service or “booking” fee, a currency-conversion markup, a resort or facility fee added at the property, or an inflated reference price designed to make a discount look bigger than it is.

None of these make your stay better. They’re a cost of the booking layer sitting between you and the hotel - and they’re often not obvious until the final screen.

Why booking direct usually avoids them

Booking directly with the property (or through a service that passes you the direct rate) removes the large commission the big booking sites take - commonly 15-25% of the room price. Less markup in the middle means the price you see is closer to what the room actually costs.

Direct bookings also make changes and cancellations simpler, and often unlock member or loyalty rates that the public OTA price can’t match.

How to check you’re getting a fair price

Confirm the total price - taxes and fees included - is shown before you pay, not revealed at the last step. Check whether the rate is refundable and what the cancellation deadline is. And compare the rate you’re offered to the hotel’s typical selling price so you can see the real difference, rather than a made-up “was” number.

Frequently asked questions

Are hotel booking fees legal in Australia?

Yes, but under Australian Consumer Law prices must not be misleading and any unavoidable fees should be included in the advertised price. Watch for fees only revealed at checkout, and prefer providers that show the all-in total up front.

What is a resort fee?

A daily charge some properties add for facilities like Wi-Fi, pools or gym access. It’s separate from the room rate. Always check whether a resort or facility fee applies before booking.

Is booking direct always cheaper?

Not always, but it frequently is once OTA commissions and fees are stripped out - and member/loyalty rates can push it lower. The key is price transparency: see the direct rate next to the typical price and judge for yourself.

How do I avoid currency-conversion fees?

Book in the local currency (AUD in Australia) and pay with a card that doesn’t charge foreign-transaction fees. OzBookings prices in AUD so there’s no surprise conversion markup.
