Book direct vs the big booking sites: what actually saves money

Is it really cheaper to book direct, or are Booking.com, Expedia and Agoda the better deal? Here’s the honest version.

How the big booking sites make money

Online travel agencies (OTAs) charge hotels a commission on every booking — commonly 15–25%. They spend heavily on advertising to win your click, and that cost is ultimately baked into room prices across the market.

They’re genuinely useful for browsing and comparing breadth of choice. But that convenience has a price, and it’s the hotel — and often you — who pays it.

When booking direct wins

Direct booking tends to win on price transparency, member and loyalty rates, more flexible cancellation, and a direct line to the property if plans change. Because there’s no commission layer, the direct rate is often at or below the OTA price — and loyalty pricing can go lower still.

When an OTA might still make sense

To be fair: OTAs shine for discovering options across many hotels at once, occasional aggressive promotions, and their own points ecosystems. If you value one loyalty programme across thousands of hotels, that has real worth. The smart move is to compare — then book wherever the true, all-in price is best.

Frequently asked questions

Is it cheaper to book direct than on Booking.com?

Often, once commissions and fees are removed — and member rates can beat the public price. It varies by hotel and date, so the best approach is to compare the direct rate against the typical price before you book.

Do I lose anything by booking direct?

Rarely. You keep free cancellation where offered, deal directly with the property for changes, and can access loyalty rates. You give up cross-hotel OTA points programmes if those matter to you.

Can I still get free cancellation booking direct?

Yes — free cancellation depends on the rate, not the channel. OzBookings flags refundable rates and shows the cancellation deadline before you pay.

Are member rates worth signing up for?

If you travel more than once or twice a year, usually yes — member (closed-user-group) rates are below the public price on eligible stays and a free account takes seconds.
